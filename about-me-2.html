About Me

 

I'm a Senior at Purdue University pursuing dual degrees in Computer Engineering and Mathematics with a minor in Psychology. I'm interested how we do computing. For too long we've thought of computers as machines that execute line after line of code. This runs counter to the spatial and nonlinear way that our universe works. Can we change our hardware, software, and interactions with machines to overcome the bottleneck of sequential processing? 

I've held internship positions in embedded software at Eaton Corporation and hardware at Qualcomm Technologies, Inc. My research experience spans math, scientific simulation, and AI (Reinforcement learning, computer vision, and SLAM), while my portfolio is full of RTL hardware design, compilers, networks, and machine learning projects.

Check out my resume and transcript for more information on my projects and coursework. At Qualcomm, I've taken additional courses in GPGPU programming, AI, manycore systems, security, asynchronous logic, and operating systems.

I'm currently looking to enter a PhD program studying Computer Science and/or Computer Engineering, and am looking for job and/or internship opportunities. If you'd like to discuss with me, contact me here!

 

When I'm not contemplating the how to revolutionize computing, you can find me playing guitar, running, or reading.
